The Liberty foundation is defined by a desire for people to have free autonomy over their lives without coercion.

1. Mentally-sound adults should have the legal right to do what they want to their own bodies, even when it may be detrimental to their health (such as refusing vaccination, or consuming hard drugs).

2. It is unethical for the government to place restrictions on what ideologies, faiths, beliefs, and opinions can and cannot be expressed within social media and academia.

3. Protecting the public’s right to privacy from surveillance and espionage should be a greater priority than catching terrorists or criminals.

4. Democracy, where leadership is determined by public election in which every citizen has a right to vote, is universally preferable to any other method of state governance.

5. All people should have a right to reproduce, even those with serious inheritable diseases.

6. The government should be prohibited from seizing the personal property of a law abiding citizen (such as a house or small business) for state planning without their consent, even if dues are paid in compensation.

7. Responsible citizens who are not members of the military or police force should have legal access to firearms for self defense.

8. Even during times of crisis such as wars or pandemics, citizens should never be denied the right to nonviolently assemble and protest in public spaces.
