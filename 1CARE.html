The Care foundation is defined by a desire to minimize the suffering of others, which manifests in notions of kindness, passivity, generosity, mercy, and acceptance.

1. When raising a child, it should be a bigger priority for them to be kind and humble than to be successful or get ahead in life.

2. Providing negative feedback to peers should typically be done softly and diplomatically, rather than blunt and upfront.

3. Those who have earned positions of high privilege and wealth should have a moral obligation to assist less fortunate members of their society

4. There are certain punishments that are so cruel and violent, that even the most evil and dangerous individuals do not deserve them.

5. A social misfit who is weak and timid, but well-intended should be treated with the same courtesy and respect as their peers, and stood up for by them when bullied by others

6. There is no end-goal noble enough, and no circumstance desperate enough to ever justify sacrificing the lives of unwilling innocents.

7. It is preferable for a society to promote people to talk openly about their mental health issues rather than to repress them.

8. It is never justifiable to declare offensive war or exert military pressure on a country that has not harmed or threatened another.
