The Fairness foundation is defined by a desire for people to be held accountable for their actions in an equal playing field. It typically manifests through concepts of justice, reciprocity, honesty, integrity, merit, and equality.

1. Neither the race, ethnicity, or caste one is born into should dictate how that person is treated by the society they grow up in.

2. Men and women should share equal rights.

3. When a friend is involved in a conflict with someone else, it is more important to support whoever is morally in the right than it is to support whoever you are personally closer to

4. It is unsettling when someone inherits a large sum of wealth or influence without having to work for it themselves.

5. A poor individual who is mentally and physically capable of working or educating themselves but refuses to do so should be shamed for involuntary means of income (such as through theft, scams, or applying for public funds)

6. All citizens convicted of a crime, no matter how obviously guilty they are, should have the right to a fair trial and due process

7. The leaders of society should be held accountable to the same standards as those they govern over.

8. It is important for all people to have integrity: reciprocating favors that are done for you later down the line when doing so is not expected, and owning up to your mistakes even when nobody would have known otherwise.
